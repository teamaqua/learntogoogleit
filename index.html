Did you know that Google searches for more than web pages? You can search for directions, pictures, videos, news, stocks, scholarly articles, and even more!

Not sure what you're looking for? Google can help you with the wildcard function. For example, searching for Lord of the * will show web pages about Lord of the Rings and Lord of the Flies.

Want to search for one word or another? That's no problem with Google. Just use the OR command: searching for dollar OR euro shows web pages containing the word dollar, or the word euro.

Want to remove words from your search? Just use the minus (-) symbol. Searching for banana -fruit shows web pages with the word banana, but no web pages will contain the word fruit (Here's an example of such a page.).

Searching for song lyrics? Or more information about a phrase? Google can help with the exact phrase search. Just encase your search with apostrophes: "in the nick of time". Can you guess the song with the line "be our guest"?

Trying to find synonyms? Google uses the ~ sign to search for synonym words. Searching for ~clock will also show pages about time.

You can search a specific site using the site: command. For example, searching for jobs site:google.com searches Google.com for job information.

Want to search within a number range? Try using the ... function. For instance, searching for $100...$300 billion shows pages containing numbers from $100 to $300 billion.

Trying to remember a certain web address? Only remember a fragment of it? Google can help you remember it. Just use the inurl: function; Googling for inurl:video will show video.nytimes.com and cnn.com/video.

Looking for a particular document? Perhaps a .PDF? Or a .DOC? You can search the whole Internet with a Google filetype: search. If you wanted to look for PDF files about Google, just search for google filetype:pdf.

Your favorite web site down? Use the cache: operator to see Google's copy of it. Here's Google's copy of Amazon's home page: cache:amazon.com.

Looking for similar sites? Use the related: command. For example, a Google search for related:ebay.com shows Amazon and Best Buy as similar sites.

Want to find more information on a web site? Google makes it easy with the info: command. Try it here: info:twitter.com.

Need to do some quick math? Just pop it into the Google search box. Try these: 2 + 4, 10 - 5, 2 * 10, or 30 / 15.

Need to convert between measurements? Type the conversion into Google: 15 feet to meters, 6 pounds to kilograms, or 2 dollars to euros.

Want to see Google do a barrel roll? Just search Google for do a barrel roll. Or you can just tilt Google a bit.

Need a word definition? Ask Google to define it with define:. Try finding the definition of eclipse with define:eclipse.

Interested in finance? Then you'll love Google Finance. Just type a stock symbol into Google search, and see information about a company. Here's stock information about Google: goog.

Need the time? Ask Google. Just search for time cityname See the current time in these major cities: Paris, London, Chicago, Orlando, New York, San Francisco, or Shanghai.

Ever wondered how many horns are on a unicorn? Google has you covered with this: number of horns on a unicorn.
